<p><p>Fresh yeast comes in two primary market types: High Sugar Type and Low Sugar Type. High Sugar Type yeast is best suited for recipes with high sugar content, as it can withstand the high levels of sweetness without losing its leavening ability. On the other hand, Low Sugar Type yeast is better for recipes with lower sugar content, as it is more efficient at fermenting simple sugars. Both types of fresh yeast are essential for bakers and chefs who need precise control over the fermentation process in their recipes.</p></p>
