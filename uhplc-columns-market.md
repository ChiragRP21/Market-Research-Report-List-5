<p><p>UHPLC columns are commonly classified into two main types: packed columns and capillary columns. Packed columns consist of small particles packed into a column, providing higher resolving power and efficiency. Capillary columns, on the other hand, are narrower in diameter and offer improved sensitivity and peak capacity. Both types of columns are vital in UHPLC systems for achieving fast separations and high-quality analytical results. Researchers often choose between packed and capillary columns based on their specific analytical needs and the characteristics of the compounds being analyzed.</p></p>
