<p><p>Ultraviolet (UV) sensors are categorized into three main types: UVA sensors, UVB sensors, and UVC sensors. UVA sensors are designed to detect UVA radiation, primarily from the sun, which is responsible for skin aging and eye damage. UVB sensors specifically measure UVB radiation, which is linked to sunburn and skin cancer. UVC sensors are utilized for detecting the most harmful type of UV radiation, UVC radiation, which is usually absorbed by the Earth's atmosphere but can be emitted artificially in certain situations.</p></p>
