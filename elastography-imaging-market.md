<p><ul><li>Ultrasound</li><li>Magnetic Resonance Elastography</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Elastography imaging is a non-invasive technique used to assess the stiffness of tissues in the body. There are two main types of elastography imaging: ultrasound elastography and magnetic resonance elastography. </p><p>Ultrasound elastography utilizes ultrasound waves to measure the stiffness of tissues, providing real-time images of tissue elasticity. This technique is commonly used in the evaluation of liver fibrosis and breast lesions.</p><p>On the other hand, magnetic resonance elastography uses magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) to map tissue stiffness, offering high-resolution images and quantitative measurements of tissue elasticity. This technique is useful in assessing liver fibrosis, brain tumors, and musculoskeletal disorders.</p></p>
