<p><p>Integral driven axle refers to a single unit where the axle and differential are combined into one component, offering simplicity and cost-effectiveness. On the other hand, split driven axle involves separate components for the axle and differential, providing flexibility for customization and maintenance. Both types cater to different market needs, with integral axles being more commonly used in mass-produced vehicles for their efficiency, while split axles are preferred in specialized or customized applications where specific requirements need to be met.</p></p>
