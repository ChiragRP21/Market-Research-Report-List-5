<p><p>Permanent pacemakers are medical devices used to regulate abnormal heart rhythms. There are three main types based on the number of chambers they work in: single-chamber, dual-chamber, and three-chamber/biventricular pacemakers. Single-chamber pacemakers have one lead in either the atrium or ventricle, dual-chamber pacemakers have leads in both the atrium and ventricle, and three-chamber pacemakers have an additional lead in the left ventricle for biventricular pacing. These different types cater to specific patient needs and conditions.</p></p>
