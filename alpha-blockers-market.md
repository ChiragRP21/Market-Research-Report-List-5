<p><p>Alpha blockers are drugs that relax certain muscles and help small blood vessels remain open. Non-selective antagonists block both Alpha-1 and Alpha-2 receptors, resulting in more side effects.Selective antagonists target only Alpha-1 receptors, leading to fewer side effects. Non-selective antagonists act more broadly in the body, affecting a wider range of functions, while selective antagonists target specific receptors, leading to more targeted therapeutic effects. Understanding the differences between these two types of alpha blockers is crucial for selecting the most appropriate treatment for each patient.</p></p>
