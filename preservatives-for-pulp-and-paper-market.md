<p><p>Preservatives for pulp and paper are classified into two main types: oxidizing and non-oxidizing. Oxidizing preservatives work by releasing active oxygen species that damage microbial cells, while non-oxidizing preservatives prevent microbial growth through other mechanisms such as disrupting cell membranes or enzymes. Oxidizing preservatives are more commonly used in applications where water clarity is important, while non-oxidizing preservatives are preferred for systems sensitive to oxidative reactions. Both types play a crucial role in extending the shelf life and quality of pulp and paper products.</p></p>
