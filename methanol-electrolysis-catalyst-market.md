<p><p>Methanol electrolysis catalysts can be categorized into three main types: zeolites, metals, and chemical compounds. Zeolites are crystalline aluminosilicates with unique pore structures that can enhance the efficiency of methanol electrolysis. Metals like nickel and copper are commonly used as catalysts due to their high conductivity and reactivity. Chemical compounds, such as tungsten carbide and molybdenum sulfide, can also serve as effective catalysts for methanol electrolysis. Each type of catalyst offers different advantages and can significantly impact the performance of the electrolysis process.</p></p>
