<p><p>Air-Entraining Admixtures without Chloride are used in concrete mixes to produce stable air bubbles without the use of chloride ions, making them suitable for projects where chloride contamination is a concern. On the other hand, Air-Entraining Admixtures with Chloride incorporate chloride ions into the mix to enhance the air-entrainment process, making the concrete more workable and durable. Both types of admixtures help improve freeze-thaw resistance, reduce bleed water, and increase cohesion in concrete mixes.</p></p>
