<p><p>Diesel lubricity improvers are additives used to enhance the lubricating properties of diesel fuel. There are two main types of lubricity improvers on the market: acidic and non-acidic. Acidic lubricity improvers work by chemically reacting with the metal surfaces to reduce friction, while non-acidic lubricity improvers create a protective film on the metal surfaces to prevent wear and tear. Both types of lubricity improvers aim to improve the efficiency and longevity of diesel engines by reducing friction and increasing lubrication.</p></p>
