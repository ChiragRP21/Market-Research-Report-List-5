<p><p>Soluble support materials for 3D printing typically fall into two main market types: PVA (polyvinyl alcohol) and HIPS (high-impact polystyrene). PVA is commonly used for printing intricate and delicate designs, as it dissolves easily in water. HIPS, on the other hand, is known for its strength and durability, making it suitable for more robust support structures. Other types of soluble support materials may include materials like PLA and nylon, each offering unique properties for different 3D printing applications.</p></p>
